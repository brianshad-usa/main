Is Your LA Business Ready for AI? Checklist

By Brian Shad  ·  Pro Link Systems  ·  July 21, 2026

Artificial intelligence is transforming how businesses operate, and Los Angeles companies are eager to stay competitive by adopting AI tools. However, rushing into AI implementation without proper preparation can lead to security vulnerabilities, integration challenges, and wasted investment. Before your business embraces AI technology, it's essential to evaluate whether your current IT infrastructure and processes can support it effectively.

At Pro Link Systems, we've been providing managed IT services to Los Angeles businesses since 1999, and we're now helping companies navigate the AI revolution. Based on our experience supporting hundreds of local businesses, we've developed this comprehensive AI readiness checklist to help you determine if your organization is prepared for successful AI adoption.

Evaluate Your Data Infrastructure and Quality

AI systems are only as effective as the data they're trained on and work with. Before implementing AI solutions, your Los Angeles business needs a solid data foundation.

Key considerations for data readiness:

  • Data organization: Is your business data centralized and easily accessible? AI tools need structured, organized data to function effectively. If your data is scattered across multiple systems or stored inconsistently, you'll need to address this first.
  • Data quality: AI algorithms can amplify existing data problems. Ensure your data is accurate, complete, and regularly updated before feeding it into AI systems.
  • Storage capacity: AI applications often require substantial storage for both training data and ongoing operations. Evaluate whether your current cloud services infrastructure can handle increased data demands.
  • Data governance: Establish clear policies about data ownership, access permissions, and usage guidelines to prevent AI systems from accessing sensitive information inappropriately.

Assess Your Cybersecurity Posture

AI adoption introduces new security considerations that Los Angeles businesses must address proactively. AI systems can become targets for cyberattacks, and poorly secured AI tools may create vulnerabilities in your network.

Security essentials before implementing AI:

  • Network security: Ensure your network has robust firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and regular security audits. AI tools that connect to the internet need strong protective barriers.
  • Access controls: Implement multi-factor authentication and role-based access controls to prevent unauthorized users from manipulating AI systems or accessing AI-generated insights.
  • Compliance requirements: If your business handles sensitive information like healthcare data, ensure your AI implementation maintains HIPAA compliance and other regulatory standards.
  • Vendor security: When selecting AI solutions, thoroughly vet vendors' cybersecurity practices and understand how they protect your data.

Pro Link Systems recommends conducting a comprehensive security assessment before AI adoption to identify and address potential vulnerabilities.

Review Your IT Infrastructure and Support Capabilities

AI applications often require more computing power, bandwidth, and technical support than traditional business software. Your IT infrastructure must be capable of handling these increased demands.

Infrastructure considerations:

  • Computing resources: Many AI applications require significant processing power. Evaluate whether your current hardware can handle AI workloads or if cloud-based solutions would be more appropriate.
  • Network bandwidth: AI tools, especially those involving real-time data processing or video analysis, consume substantial bandwidth. Ensure your internet connection can support these requirements without impacting other business operations.
  • Integration capabilities: Can AI tools integrate smoothly with your existing business applications? Poor integration leads to data silos and inefficiencies that undermine AI's benefits.
  • IT support readiness: Does your internal IT team or IT support provider have the expertise to troubleshoot AI-related issues? AI technologies require specialized knowledge that traditional IT support may lack.
  • Backup and recovery: Implement robust disaster recovery plans that account for AI systems and the critical data they rely on.

Prepare Your Team and Processes

Technology is only part of the AI readiness equation. Your Los Angeles business also needs organizational readiness to maximize AI's value.

Organizational readiness factors:

  • Skills assessment: Identify skill gaps within your team and plan for training or hiring to fill them. Successful AI adoption requires people who understand both the technology and your business processes.
  • Change management: Prepare employees for how AI will change their workflows. Address concerns transparently and highlight how AI will augment rather than replace human capabilities.
  • Clear objectives: Define specific, measurable goals for AI implementation. Are you trying to improve customer service, streamline operations, or enhance decision-making? Clear objectives guide vendor selection and implementation priorities.
  • Budget planning: Beyond initial software costs, consider ongoing expenses for subscriptions, infrastructure upgrades, training, and additional IT support Los Angeles businesses typically need.

Take the Next Step Toward AI Readiness

AI represents an enormous opportunity for Los Angeles businesses, but successful implementation requires careful preparation. By working through this checklist, you can identify gaps in your current technology infrastructure and create a roadmap for addressing them before investing in AI solutions.

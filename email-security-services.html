Why It Matters

The inbox is the front door attackers use most

Email remains the most dangerous attack surface for any business. Phishing campaigns, business email compromise (BEC), and executive impersonation are responsible for billions in annual losses — and unlike malware, these attacks often bypass standard filters because they exploit trust, not code. A single convincing email to the right employee can trigger a fraudulent wire transfer, expose sensitive credentials, or install ransomware with one click.

Pro Link Systems takes a multi-layer approach to email security. We combine enterprise-grade filtering that blocks malicious content at delivery with authentication standards that prevent criminals from spoofing your domain to impersonate your executives and vendors. For organizations handling high-value transactions, we add advanced sandboxing and real-time BEC detection — the exact defenses that stop the targeted attacks behind most wire-fraud incidents.

Every solution we deploy is fully managed: we configure it, monitor it, and adjust it as the threat landscape evolves — so your team doesn't need to become email security experts.

Common Questions

Email security — answered

Business email compromise is a targeted attack where criminals impersonate executives, vendors, or business partners — typically to redirect a payment, obtain sensitive data, or manipulate an employee into taking a harmful action. Unlike mass phishing, BEC is carefully crafted and often uses no malicious links or attachments, making it difficult for basic filters to catch. We stop BEC with a combination of advanced behavioral analysis (Proofpoint Advanced Plus), DMARC/DKIM/SPF authentication to block domain spoofing, and security awareness training to help your team recognize impersonation tactics.
DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting & Conformance) is an email authentication standard that tells receiving mail servers what to do when email claims to come from your domain but fails authentication checks — typically to quarantine or reject it. Without DMARC, anyone can send an email that appears to come from your company's domain. We configure DMARC alongside DKIM and SPF, monitor reporting data, and gradually move you to enforcement (p=reject) — the only policy that fully prevents domain spoofing.
Proofpoint Professional provides enterprise-grade inbound and outbound filtering — blocking spam, phishing, and malware-laden attachments at delivery. Proofpoint Advanced Plus adds real-time URL and attachment sandboxing (detonating suspicious content in a safe environment before it's delivered) and dedicated BEC and executive impersonation protection. For businesses handling financial transactions, high-value wire transfers, or sensitive client data, we recommend Advanced Plus — it specifically addresses the attack vectors most likely to result in financial loss.

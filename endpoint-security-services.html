Why It Matters

Devices are where attacks land — and where you stop them

Endpoints — laptops, desktops, and servers — are the most common target in modern cyberattacks. Ransomware typically enters through a phishing email or unpatched vulnerability, executes on a device, and then moves laterally across the network to encrypt everything it can reach. The window between initial infection and full impact is often measured in minutes. Legacy antivirus misses the threats that matter most.

Pro Link Systems deploys next-generation endpoint detection and response (EDR) powered by AI that analyzes behavior in real time — not just signatures. When SentinelOne detects suspicious activity, it automatically isolates the affected device and kills the process before the attack can spread. Paired with our 24/7 Security Operations Center, every alert is reviewed by a human analyst who makes the call to remediate, escalate, or clear the event.

We layer this with baseline protections — Microsoft Defender for daily threat hygiene, BitLocker encryption for lost or stolen devices, and patch management to close the vulnerabilities attackers actively scan for. The result is a complete endpoint security program, fully managed, with no gaps.

Common Questions

Endpoint security — answered

Traditional antivirus detects known threats by matching files against a database of signatures — it misses new, unknown, or fileless attacks entirely. Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) monitors behavior in real time: what processes are running, what files they're touching, what network connections they're making, and whether that behavior looks like an attack — regardless of whether the threat has been seen before. EDR also records a forensic timeline so we can understand exactly what happened during an incident. We use EDR as the primary defense for all monitored endpoints.
A SOC is a team of analysts who monitor your environment around the clock — reviewing security alerts, investigating suspicious events, and responding to incidents as they happen. Attacks don't observe business hours; many ransomware deployments are intentionally triggered overnight or on weekends when IT teams are offline. Our 24/7 SOC means a human is watching your endpoints at 2am on a Saturday — and can act within minutes of a confirmed threat rather than hours or days later.

If ransomware executes on a protected endpoint, SentinelOne's automated response immediately isolates the device from the network (stopping lateral spread), kills the malicious process, and can roll back encrypted files to their pre-attack state using the Storyline™ engine — in many cases without any data loss. Our SOC analysts are simultaneously alerted and take over remediation. For persistence across the business, our BCDR program provides immutable backups as a final recovery option.
